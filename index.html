The Brain

MOTHERBOARD

Motherboard

The motherboard is like the brain's CPU, responsible for coordinating and managing all the operations of your computer.
It controls the flow of information between the different components, much like the brain processes and directs signals between different areas.
It's basically the foundation that holds everything together and makes sure your computer can run smoothly.

The Visionary Artist

Graphical Processing Unit

Graphics Card

A GPU, or Graphics Processing Unit, is like a super-fast brain for handling graphics and other tasks on your computer.
It helps make things like games and videos look smooth and realistic.
Plus, it's really good at crunching numbers quickly, which makes it handy for lots of other stuff like science and art too!

The Powerhouse

Power Supply Unit

Power Supply Unit

Think of a Power Supply Unit (PSU) as the heart of your computer's power system.
It's the box inside your computer that takes electricity from your wall outlet and converts
it into the right voltage and current to power all the components in your computer, like the motherboard, CPU, GPU, and drives.

Storage House

SSD/HDD

Storage units

SSD (Solid State Drive): Makes your computer faster by storing data on flash memory, leading to quicker boot-ups, faster program launches, and snappier performance overall.
HDD (Hard Disk Drive): Offers ample storage space at a lower cost, ideal for storing large files like videos and photos, ensuring you have plenty of room for your data.
